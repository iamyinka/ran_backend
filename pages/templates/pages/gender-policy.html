Gender Policy

RedAid Nigeria (RAN) is dedicated to empowering individuals affected by leprosy, TB, Buruli Ulcer, and other NTDs. They focus particularly on women who play an important and equal role in community and RAN achievements. The organization has a gender-sensitive approach in line with the Federal Ministry of Women Affairs of Nigeria's vision to eliminate gender discrimination and guarantee equal access to opportunities.

RAN's goal is to promote gender equity and equality within the organization and eliminate all forms of discrimination against women. They aim to provide gender-sensitive direction to all their staff in policy-making, planning, designing, and implementing all development projects.

RAN recognizes that gender equality is the key to sustainable development and aims to prioritize gender issues in all their development activities. The organization aims to support women in developing their skills, self-confidence, and self-reliance. They also have a code of conduct that prohibits sexual exploitation and abuse of any person, especially children. The organization conducts gender analyses throughout the project cycle to examine the differences between the roles that women, men, boys, and girls play, their power levels, needs, constraints, and opportunities, and develop strategies to redress gender inequities.

RAN ensures that males and females have equal opportunities to participate and that interviewers are proportionate to the number and sex of participants
