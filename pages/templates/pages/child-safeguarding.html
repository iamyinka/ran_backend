Child Safeguarding Policy: Protecting Children from Abuse and Exploitation.

This document outlines the key definitions and commitments of the Child Safeguarding Policy of RedAid Nigeria (RAN).

RAN's Child Safeguarding Policy applies to all employees, project partners, consultants, service providers, interns, and volunteers, as well as anyone traveling to project sites on behalf of RAN. It standardizes child protection measures within the organization and funded projects, aiming to minimize the risk of violence and abuse.

Child: For the purpose of this policy, a child refers to anyone under the age of 18, in accordance with the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child.

In the case of zoonoses such as Chagas , Buruli ulcer or tuberculosis - infectious diseases that can be transmitted from animals to humans - we want to increasingly rely on the interdisciplinary approach "One Health", with actors from human, veterinary and Environmental sciences collaborate. In addition, the transversal “empowerment” sector is playing an increasingly important role: the empowerment or self-empowerment of people on an individual, community and national level.

Child Abuse: The World Health Organization defines child abuse as any form of physical and/or emotional abuse, sexual abuse, neglect, and commercial or other exploitation that results in actual or potential harm to the health, development, or dignity of the child within the framework of a relationship of responsibility, trust, or power.

Participation of Children: The organization is committed to developing frameworks that empower children to represent their interests, where their perspectives are valued.

Child Protection: It is a broad term used to describe policies, standards, and procedures for preventing and protecting children from abuse. The organization's child protection policy applies to all projects in which adults have direct or indirect contact with children. The policy ensures that measures are taken to prevent and combat abuse and incidents are analyzed to improve child protection.

Declaration of Commitment: The organization commits to preventing and protecting children from abuse and exploitation, supporting their rights, and creating a safe environment. It also raises awareness of child protection, develops and implements measures to prevent abuse, and sensitizes relevant interest groups.

Actors Commitment: Actors in the scope of the Child Safeguarding Directive commit to ensuring visual contact during interactions with children, complying with the RAN Code of Conduct, not tolerating or encouraging abusive behavior towards children, and preventing those convicted of child abuse from being involved in the program.

Communication Standards: The organization takes measures to protect the child's identity and dignity in all media content, avoiding stereotypes and portraying children as multifaceted individuals. It also obtains consent from children and their parents/guardians before using their photographs or reports.

Implementation: The organization distributes its guidelines to employees and partners, attaches them to contracts, explains them to employees, and ensures all parties comply. The child protection team examines all reports, takes appropriate measures, and communicates with stakeholders about the reporting channels.

In summary, the organization is committed to protecting children from abuse, supporting their rights, and creating a safe environment. It implements guidelines and standards to ensure all employees, partners, and actors comply with its Child Safeguarding Policy.
